About myself

In short: I'm 23 and I graduated from university in May 2011. I have been doing this web thing fairly consistently from when I was about 12. I consider myself more user interface focused, though I enjoy everything about the design process. I aspire to be half decent at illustration, someday…

Skills

Photoshop
Although it's use isn't as prolific as it used to be, it's still a part of my workflow. You can't beat it for quickly experimenting with ideas and getting the larger part of the way a website should look and feel.
HTML
The starting point for the coding side any website is generally getting into the HTML. So it's obvious HTML should be in my skillset! I feel like this should be a given, but yes that includes HTML5 elements.
CSS
The stuff that makes a website come to life. I try to take a DRY approach to my CSS. I use Sass which I literally can't live without now. Another given I feel is I have great knowledge of CSS3. This means little for clients, but it helps me get my job done better.
JavaScript/jQuery
Always an area I'm looking to improve. I use it to enhance websites where it works best, be it a slider or something as simple as lettering.js.
ExpressionEngine
A CMS I adore, powerful in what it can do and a CMS which doesn't get in my way. It doesn't decide anything for me and lets me have full control over how I write the HTML. Which in turn helps me develop websites quicker! Also has a brilliant community behind it, you certainly get what you pay for.
